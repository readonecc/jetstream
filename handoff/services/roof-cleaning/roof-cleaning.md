## Section 5: Category Overview

**H2**: Why Roof Cleaning Matters in Brisbane

Brisbane's subtropical climate creates the perfect conditions for mould, moss, lichen, and algae to take hold on your roof. With warm temperatures, high humidity, and regular rainfall, biological growth can spread quickly — especially on shaded sections and south-facing surfaces. Left untreated, this buildup doesn't just look bad. It traps moisture against your roofing materials, accelerates wear, and can lead to costly damage over time.

Professional roof cleaning removes this growth and restores your roof's appearance, but the right approach depends on your roof type and condition. That's why we offer three distinct roof cleaning methods — soft washing, pressure washing, and biocide treatment — each designed for different situations and surfaces. Soft washing is the safest option for most roofs, using low pressure and specialised solutions to break down organic growth without risking damage. Pressure washing is suited to harder surfaces like concrete tiles where a more aggressive clean is needed. And biocide treatment can be applied after cleaning to kill growth at the root and keep your roof cleaner for longer.

These services work together. Many of our Brisbane clients combine a soft wash or pressure wash with a biocide treatment for the best possible result — a roof that's not only clean today, but stays that way for months to come. Whether you've got a Colorbond roof, concrete tiles, or terracotta, we'll recommend the right method for your property.

At Jetstream Pressure Washing AU, we're a local, family-owned business with hundreds of 5-star reviews across Brisbane. Every job is backed by $20 million in public liability insurance, a 100% money-back guarantee, and payment only on completion. If you're not sure which roof cleaning service is right for you, get in touch for a free quote and we'll point you in the right direction.

## Section 13: FAQ Section

- **Kicker**: GOT QUESTIONS?
- **H2**: Frequently Asked Questions
- **Subtitle**: Common questions about our roof cleaning services in Brisbane.

**Q1**: What's the difference between roof soft washing and roof pressure washing?
**A1**: Soft washing uses low pressure combined with specialised cleaning solutions to safely remove mould, algae, and lichen. It's the preferred method for most roof types including Colorbond, painted metal, and delicate tiles. Pressure washing uses controlled high pressure and is best suited to hard-wearing surfaces like concrete tiles where stubborn buildup needs a more aggressive clean. We assess your roof and recommend the right approach every time.

**Q2**: What is a biocide roof treatment and do I need one?
**A2**: A biocide treatment is a professional-grade solution applied to your roof after cleaning. It kills mould, moss, lichen, and algae at the root and creates a protective barrier that slows regrowth. It's especially useful in Brisbane's humid climate where biological growth returns quickly. We recommend it for any roof that's prone to recurring buildup.

**Q3**: How often should I have my roof cleaned?
**A3**: In Brisbane, we generally recommend having your roof professionally cleaned every 12 to 24 months, depending on your roof type, surrounding tree coverage, and how much shade the roof gets. Properties with heavy shade or overhanging trees may need more frequent cleaning to stay on top of mould and moss growth.

**Q4**: Is roof cleaning safe for Colorbond and tile roofs?
**A4**: Absolutely. We tailor our method and pressure to suit your roof material. Colorbond and painted metal roofs are cleaned using our soft wash system to avoid any risk of damage or coating loss. Concrete and terracotta tiles can handle higher pressure where needed. We never use more pressure than the surface requires.

**Q5**: Will walking on my roof cause damage?
**A5**: Our team is trained to work safely on all roof types, and we take every precaution to avoid damage. We use appropriate footwear, distribute our weight carefully, and avoid walking on ridges and fragile areas. For delicate or older roofs, we use extended reach equipment to minimise foot traffic wherever possible.
